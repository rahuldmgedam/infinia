What is WPC

Nations around the globe including India, are moving towards sustainable products, which are durable, easy to work with, cost-effective and are environment friendly at the same time.

Wood Polymer Composite is one such material, which is manufactured using plant based natural fibers and wood waste along with polymers, which enhances specific properties such as durability, strength, thermal behavior, recyclability amongst others, to prolong the life and upgrade the quality of the products..

Benefits of WPC

  • Combines the best properties of both wood and polymers.
  • Resistant to rot, decay and insect damage, products last longer than traditional wood products and require less maintenance.
  • Resistant to fading, cracking, and warping, even when exposed to extreme temperatures and weather conditions.
  • Can be designed to mimic the look and feel of natural wood, while offering the benefits of a composite material
  • Longer lifespan and require less maintenance, making them a cost-effective choice in the long run.

    PRODUCTION METHOD

    WPC Board are produced by means of the extrusion method. Sheet extrusion is a thermoplastic processing method.
    A board extrusion plant consists of an extruder,a tool sometimes referred to as a die with a fishtail design for shaping purposes, a triple roller calendar to smooth the extruded material, a cooling section, a haul-off and a separating unit (saw or cutting device) and a destacker.

    By applying heat, shearing and pressure, the raw material is heated during the extrusion process to convert it into a viscious fluid form. A rotating screw presses this molten material through a shaping tool-fishtail nozzle. The surface of the calendar rollers generally has a highgloss finish. It essentially determines the surface quality of the sheet. The shaping process is complete once the calendar has performed its smoothening task. After the extruded material has cooled down on the roller conveyor, sheets are produced by being cut to the desired length in the separating unit before being off-loaded onto transport pallets by the destacker. As the material meets the aforementioned requirements, Alstone Industries IM is suitable for boat- and ship building purposes and can be used on bulkheads, walls, ceilings, linings or floor coverings. In the event of a fire, hydrogen chloride gas and other broken down byproducts may be released. For this reason, Alstone WPC Boards should not be burnt without taking suitable emission control precautions. Offcuts and/or sanding dust, as with any other thermo plastic material,should be properly disposed of. Please follow the instructions of your local disposal expert.

    Performance in outdoor use

    WPC Board is extremely resistant to external influences such as sun, rain, snow, or salt water. As the WPC  Hybrid material is also UV resistant, Alstone Industries is suitable for outdoor use. For optimal protection and to avoid bleaching of the material due to natural causes, we generally recommend the application of a varnish or sealant.
    Temperature changes can alter the dimensions of any material. For this reason, the thermal expansion coefficient should be taken Into account when cutting and assembling Alstone WPC Board. The thermal expansion coefficient of plastics is significantly greater than that of metal or wood and must therefore- be taken Into account when Alstone WPC is used in areas that are subject to a high degree of temperature fluctuation.

    Cutting and assembly of the WPC Board should be completed under stable temperature conditions. It is recommended that the material is stored in the shade or in areas with no direct sunlight before being cut or assembled, as the material can significantly heat up in the sun and expand accordingly. We recommend storing the product for at least 24 hours at a consistent temperature that corresponds to that of the subsequent area of application. Apart from its composition, the processing method, handling conditions, and design of the finished product, as well as the resultant tension exerted on it, will have an effect on the lifespan of the material. The latter is also influenced by ambient conditions as well as strains and stresses exerted during installation and processing.

    WATER ABSORPTION

    Due to the smooth surface, the organic constituents on the surface of the product can never absorb moisture. However, water can penetrate into open pores. Water absorbed in this way does not initiate any rotting processes; it can be released again during drying.

    Temperature Range

    WPC Board is based on a thermoplastic material and therefore displays the usual properties of a thermoplastic when exposed to higher temperatures. In the case of parts not subject to mechanical wear and tear, we, therefore, recommend a maximum operating temperature of 65 Degrees Celsius. Depending on the level of wear and tear, the maximum operating temperature may be lower than 65 degree Celsius.

    RESISTANCE TO MICROORGANISMS AND INSECTS

    Industries does not act as a food source for:
    • Microorganisms
    • Bacteria
    • Mould (fungi that discolor or destroys wood)
    • Gnawing insects

    Depending on the environment in which it is used, organic residues may be deposited on WPC Board. These are the ideal breeding ground for the kind of fungal spores naturally present in the ambient air and may lead to staining. However, the material itself will not deteriorate as a result. For optimal protection, we recommend cleaning Alstone wpc where required and removing any organic residues ( eg. Leaves or foliage)

    • Machining
    • Drilling

    WPC Board can be drilled using standard spiral drills. No special grinding Is generally required. However, we do recommend the use of drill bits with relief ground cutting edges and small twist angles. if the rake angle of the bit has been negatively ground for drilling holes, it prevents the bit from jamming and the material from breaking. This Is recommended up to a drill hole depth of approx. 15 mm. If the drill hole Is longer than 5 x a, it is advisable to withdraw the bit from the hole a number of times in order to extract the shavings efficiently. If the drill hole is more than 10 mm in diameter, pre-drilling is to be recommended. Drill holes exceeding 20mm in diameter can be created more efficiently using double–edged bits with a pilot, while drill holes exceeding 40mm in diameter are best made with circle cutters. The use of Forstner bits is also possible.

    Drilling chart

    Cutting speed and forward feed depends on the depth of the drill hole. If the proper processing parameters are observed, no smearing of the material will occur.

    MILLING

    All standard milling machines used in metal machining and designed for high speeds are suitable. High cutting speeds and small cutting depths are recommended.

    DRESSING/PLANING

    The edges can be planed with the usual tools and conventional planning and thickness planning machines. Manual files and handheld electric planers can also be used to dress the sheets.

    SAWING

    WPC Board can be cut lengthwise and crosswise with all the usual sawing equipment.

    Circular Saws

    To achieve clean cutting edges, the saw blade should only project slightly beyond the Alstone WPC Board in each case. The use of carbide-tipped saw blades improves cutting performance and quality, while also considerably extending the tool life of the saw blade.     Band saws

    SANDING

    WPC Boards comes smooth on both side as standard. To give the look and feel of wood, sanding of the finished product is necessary. Sanding may be done by hand or with suitable sanding machines (e.g. belt sanders), but in any event the sheets should always be sanded In the same direction (cross-wise or lengthwise). Depending on the desired surface finish, we recommend the use of sandpaper w ith a grain of 24 to 60. It Is best to begin with a coarse grain paper, followed by progressively finer sand paper until a smooth surface is achieved. Finer sandpaper should only be used to remove surface dirt. The look and feel of wood may not be achieved if the sand paper is too fine or the product is sanded using a circular motion. However, this will have no adverse effect on the quality of the product. For optimal protection of the finished surface, we recommend application of a sealant.

    JOINING - SCREW FIXING

    We recommend appropriate pre-drilling for all screw diameters.To minimise the risk of breaking around the edges,the minimum distance of 20 mm from any edge should be maintained for all screws. As a rule, the use of flathead screws is recommended.When using flat-head screws, they should be countersunk so the material is not put under pressure. Lower-stress joining can be achieved with the help of special plastic screws. Here, too, pre-drilling is esse ntial. flor further info rmation, please refer to documentation provided by the respective suppliers of screws.

    In application areas prone to tempe rature f luctuation, the thermal expansion coeff icient should be taken into account. In this case, a larger drill hole and the use of so-called plumber screws (screws with washers) may be advisable.

    RIVETING/NAILING

    We do not recommend driving any /nails into WPC Boards as this may lead to cracking or breaking of the sheets. Even predrilling the nail holes will not rule out the chance of tension cracks appearing. Depending on the specific field of application, we recommend testing the proposed method on sample sheets.

    GLUING

    WPC Boards can be glued. Due to the host of different gluing agents available and the various options for gluing Alstone WPC Boards to different kinds of surface materials, we recommend con ducting preliminary tests on sample sheets. Good results can be expected from glues (from various manufacturers) suitable for hard plastics (rigid PVC/PVC-U) .They include, for example, STPU (hybrid) adhesives, one-part adhesives and twocomponents PU/PUR adhesives as well as polyester and epoxy resins. When using adhesives it is also advisable in many cases to pre-treat the substrate with a cleaning agent or primer. For other material combinations, please speak to your pre ferred adhesive supplier or contact our Technical Service Centre. We work in close cooperation with other wellknown producers of adhesives and are committed to reviewing our knowledge continuously as regards Alstone WPC Board bonding by means of adhesives.     THERMAL PROCESSING WARM BENDING

    The linear bending zone of an WPC Boards can be heated in various ways on one or both sides:

    • Without contact
    • With heaters (infrared or quartz heaters)
    • With glow wires or heating rods
    • With a hot air blower

    General advice on storage of WPC Boards Storage should be in a suitable building, free of any moisture, temperature swings or direct sunlight. Any packaging straps should be released as soon as possible after the delivery. WPC is replaced, steel bands should be avoided wherever and whenever possible. Unilateral heating by any heat source must be avoided. Sufficient ventilation of the material must be ensured. To avoid foxing or mould stains, the material should not be wrapped in plastic film. The sheets must be stored on a stable level and on a well-supported pallet that is at least as large as the size of the sheets. Individual sheets should be stored flat and lying down. An intermediate layer (e.g. cardboard) between the pallet and the semi-finished plastic product is recommended. In case block storage of several pallets is involved, we recommend using one pallet ''head first'' as an interim layer between each pallet to ensure the weight is evenly distributed.
